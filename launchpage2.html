The National Service Scheme (NSS)

The sole aim of the NSS is to provide hands-on experience to young students in delivering community service. It's a platform for students to develop their personality while making a tangible impact on society.

Our Mission

To arouse social consciousness of the youth with an overall objective of personality development of the students through community service.

Our Vision

To build the youth with the mind and spirit to serve the society and work for the social uplift of the down-trodden masses of our nation as a movement for value based education.

Core Activities

From tree plantation drives and blood donation camps to cleanliness campaigns and educational programs in rural areas, we are constantly working towards a better tomorrow.

Our Core Objectives

Guiding principles that form the foundation of every activity we undertake.

Community Understanding

To understand the community we work in and to understand ourselves in relation to our community.

Social Responsibility

To develop a sense of social and civic responsibility among students.

Problem Solving

To identify community needs and problems and involve them in the problem-solving process.

Leadership & Democracy

To acquire leadership qualities and democratic attitudes for a better society.

Emergency Preparedness

To develop the capacity to meet emergencies and natural disasters effectively.

National Integration

To practice and promote national integration and social harmony among all communities.

Frequently Asked Questions

Have questions? We've got answers. Here are some of the most common queries we receive.

Any bonafide student of Graphic Era Hill University, Bhimtal Campus can register to become an NSS volunteer. We welcome students from all courses and years who have a passion for community service.

Besides the satisfaction of serving the community, volunteers develop leadership, teamwork, and communication skills. Active volunteers who complete the mandatory hours of service are also eligible for an NSS Certificate from the Ministry of Youth Affairs and Sports, which holds significant value.

An NSS volunteer is expected to put in at least 120 hours of service over a period of two years. This includes participation in regular activities and the Annual Special Camp. However, the true commitment is the passion you bring to the cause.
